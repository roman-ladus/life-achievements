# Life Achievements

*What does it mean to be human—according to the very things that are not?*

**[Explore the Live Gallery Here: roman-ladus.github.io/life-achievements/](https://roman-ladus.github.io/life-achievements/)**

Life Achievements is primarily an **experiment in digital philosophy and comparative AI thinking**. The objective was simple: force artificial intelligence models to simulate humanity to its core, and analyze exactly where each model places value and meaning.

## The Philosophy

We often ask AI to write code, compose emails, or summarize data. But what happens if we challenge it to understand the fundamental architecture of human existence? 

Five major language models, Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, and DeepSeek—were all given the exact same prompt: **imagine you will live exactly one human life, and design 10 achievements that are mandatory to truly understand what it means to be human.**

They were deliberately constrained:
1. No abstract fluff (e.g., "be happy"). The achievements had to be concrete and actionable.
2. They had to encompass a wide range of human experience: love, loss, risk, creation, and connection.

### The Results

The differences in their responses highlight fascinating distinctions in their core alignment and "personalities":
- **Connection vs. Creation**: Some models place immense value on building things that outlast you. Others prioritize the subtle, quiet moments of intimacy and grief.
- **Vulnerability**: Several models identified that failing publicly, and surviving the shame, is a universal and necessary human rite of passage.
- **The Physicality of Life**: Without bodies, these models consistently recognize that confronting physical nature (spending 72 hours without devices) is foundational to our species.
